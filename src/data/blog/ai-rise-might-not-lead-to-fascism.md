With the rapid advancements in artificial intelligence (AI) and the race to develop Artificial General Intelligence (AGI), concerns about AI leading to fascism have emerged. While this fear is understandable, I believe the emergence of AI is unlikely to result in a fascist world for several reasons.

## Table of contents

## Why AI might won't lead to fascism?


### AGI is Not the Sole Determinant of Corporate Dominance

Firstly, AGI alone will not determine corporate dominance. Many industries still hold competetive market cap that do not necessarily work in or require AGI. Also as the companies in diverse industries continue integrating AI for optimizating their workflows and applications wherever necessary, ensures that AI development remains distributed, preventing any single corporation or a small group of companies from monopolizing the entire AI landscape and creating a dystopian future.

### AI Development is Not Limited to Large Corporations

I believe that no single company holds a complete monopoly over all aspects of AGI development. These aspects include hardware, data availability, skilled employees, and infrastructure design. As a result, companies remain interdependent to some extent for advancing AGI.

There is a possibility that these corporations could align with a singular motivation to establish dominance. However the challenge remains for the companies, that they ensure that skilled workers remain aligned with the company's goals in a competitive, non-monopolistic market, even when their motivations change towards more radical approaches. The recent resignations of the top level management in the big tech companies, and the rise of new startups in the AI space, indicate that the market is still competitive and that no single company can dominate the entire AI landscape easily.

### Advancements in Smaller AI Models
As impressive as large language models (LLMs) are, ongoing research is focused on optimizing their capabilities for smaller models. This trend will increase accessibility to AI for individuals and smaller companies, reducing the risk of a single entity monopolizing the technology.


### AI is Not a Fundamental Human Necessity
 Unlike basic needs such as food, water, and shelter, AGI is a tool that can enhance our lives but is not essential for survival *yet*. This distinction means that people can choose to reject or resist AI-driven products or services that do not align with their values or interests.

### Is the Probability of Fascism Zero?

Well not entirely. While multiple countries and companies are competing in the development of AGI and the dominance might not just be defined by producing an AGI product, it is also true that the emergence of AI will lead to rapid unemployment creating a huge difference of economic conditions between the companies and the unemployed. Also companies that are developing AGI and reinvesting their bulk earnings iteratively in replacing the existing companies can lead to a monopoly at the end of the iteration. which could lead to a fascist dystopian world, we were talking about. However, it boils down to how do respective governments handle these scenarios in those particular countries on resisting the clear dominance of a single company or a group of companies by translating the benefits to the lower end of the economy and handling the rapid unemployment.

### What can  governments do?

#### Addressing Unemployment

Governments should proactively mitigate job displacement by:

* Encouraging workforce reskilling to focus on skilled labor rather than barrierless jobs, those could be easily replaced by AI.

* Supporting unemployed skilled workers in becoming entrepreneurs by easing business regulations and fostering AI-driven startup ecosystems.

could be some of the ways to handle the rapid unemployment.

#### Encourage Diverse AI Development
Governments should promote diverse AI development by:
* Supporting open-source AI projects and research initiatives to ensure that AI advancements are accessible to a wide range of developers and researchers.
* Encouraging collaboration between academia, industry, and government to foster innovation and prevent monopolization.
* Providing funding and resources for startups and small businesses to develop AI solutions that address specific societal needs, rather than solely focusing on profit-driven applications.
* Ensuring niche companies adapt to AI faster to prevent AI monopolies by big-tech companies.

#### Promote Ethical AI Practices
Governments should establish ethical guidelines and regulations for AI development and deployment, ensuring that AI technologies are used responsibly and transparently. This includes:
* Encouraging companies to adopt ethical AI practices, such as fairness, accountability, and transparency in their algorithms and decision-making processes.
* Supporting research on the societal impacts of AI and promoting public awareness of AI-related issues.

#### Encourage Public Discourse and Education
Governments should promote public discourse and education on AI-related topics to ensure that citizens are informed and engaged in discussions about the implications of AI. This can be achieved by:
* Supporting educational initiatives that teach AI literacy and critical thinking skills, enabling individuals to understand and navigate the complexities of AI technologies.
* Encouraging public forums, workshops, and discussions on AI-related issues to foster a culture of open dialogue and collaboration among stakeholders.
* Engaging with civil society organizations, academia, and industry to ensure that diverse perspectives are considered in AI policy-making.

### Conclusion
While AI poses challenges, it does not necessarily lead to a fascist dystopia. The accessibility of AI development, the diversification of industries, and historical patterns of societal resistance suggest that monopolization and authoritarian control are not inevitable. However, proactive governance and strategic decision-making are essential to ensure that AI benefits the interests at a social level than at an individual level.
